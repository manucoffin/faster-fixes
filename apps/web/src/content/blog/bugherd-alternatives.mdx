## 1. Faster Fixes

**TL;DR:** An open-source website feedback widget built specifically for developers — the only tool on this list with a native MCP server for AI coding agents.

**Pricing:**

- Free: $0/mo — 1 project, 50 feedback items, 1 member
- Pro: $20/mo — 5 projects, unlimited feedback, 5 members
- Agency: $99/mo — unlimited projects, unlimited feedback, unlimited members
- Self-hosted: free under AGPL-3.0 (dashboard, API, MCP server); widget packages under MIT

**Best for:** Dev agencies and solo developers who want feedback to flow directly into Claude Code, Cursor, or Windsurf — without copy-pasting context.

**Strengths:**

- **MCP server ships with the product** — connect once, your AI agent fetches new feedback, locates the component, applies the fix, marks it resolved. Fully unattended.
- **Auto-context is the deepest in this category** — every feedback item captures screenshot + URL + DOM selector + React component tree + browser environment.
- **Flat pricing** — $20/mo for a 5-person team is the lowest flat-rate option in this list by a wide margin.
- **White-label widget** included on Pro (not an Enterprise-only add-on).
- **GitHub and Linear integrations** on Pro — no Zapier dependency.
- **Self-hosted option** means zero vendor lock-in — you own your data, you control the deployment.

**Weaknesses:**

- **Newer product** — smaller community and fewer third-party integrations than BugHerd or Marker.io.
- **No session replay** — if you need to watch user recordings alongside feedback, you'll need a separate tool.
- **50-item cap on the free plan** is tight for active projects; you'll hit it during a real client review cycle.
- **No mobile SDK** — widget is web-only; not suitable for native iOS/Android feedback.

## 2. Marker.io

**TL;DR:** The most polished bug-reporting tool for teams already living in Jira, Linear, or GitHub — reporters submit feedback for free, seats are for your internal team.

**Pricing:**

- Starter: $59/mo (3 seats, 1 website, 5k pageviews/mo) · $39/mo annual
- Team: $199/mo (15 seats, 3 websites, 25k pageviews/mo) · $149/mo annual
- Agency: $129/mo (15 members, 50 websites, 50 guests) · $99/mo annual — eligibility conditions apply
- Additional seats: $9/mo monthly · $6/mo annual
- No free plan; 15-day trial

**Best for:** Agencies managing 3–50 client websites who need structured bug reports flowing directly into their existing issue tracker.

**Strengths:**

- **Reporters (clients, end-users) are free and unlimited** — they submit via widget, no account required.
- **Two-way sync with Jira, Linear, GitHub, GitLab, Asana, ClickUp, and more** — status changes in your tracker reflect back in Marker.io.
- **Session replay** captures what happened before the bug was reported.
- **Console logs and network requests** captured automatically — developers get everything they need without follow-up questions.
- **AI features** (translation, magic rewrite, title generation) on all paid plans.

**Weaknesses:**

- **No free plan** — $59/mo is the floor, which stings for small agencies.
- **Pageview caps on Starter** (5k/mo) are easy to exceed on any real production site.
- **Guests count as seats** — external collaborators who need dashboard access eat into your seat count.
- **No self-hosted option** — all data lives on Marker.io infrastructure.

## 3. Userback

**TL;DR:** A full feedback loop platform for SaaS product teams — visual feedback, session replay, NPS surveys, and feature portals in one place.

**Pricing:**

- Free: $0 — 2 projects, 2 seats, feedback expires after 7 days
- Team: $7/seat/mo annual · $9/seat/mo monthly
- Business: $15/seat/mo annual · $19/seat/mo monthly — adds session replay, console logs, network requests
- Business Plus: $23/seat/mo annual · $29/seat/mo monthly — adds mobile SDK, SSO, REST API, webhooks

**Best for:** SaaS product teams that need to close the full feedback loop — from bug report to NPS survey to feature request portal — without stitching together multiple tools.

**Strengths:**

- **Free plan exists** — 2 projects and unlimited feedback collection, though feedback expires after 7 days.
- **Session replay on Business** — watch exactly what the user did before submitting.
- **Feature portal and NPS/CSAT surveys** built in — not just bug reporting.
- **MCP server available** — Userback also ships an MCP integration for AI agent workflows.
- **SOC 2 Type II certified** — matters for enterprise buyers.

**Weaknesses:**

- **Per-seat pricing compounds** — a 10-person team on Business costs $150/mo annual; add contractors and it climbs fast.
- **Free plan feedback expires after 7 days** — useless for any real async review cycle.
- **No self-hosted option.**
- **Browser extension required** for some screenshot functionality on lower tiers — adds client onboarding friction.

## 4. Atarim

**TL;DR:** A visual collaboration platform with an AI "InnerCircle" of six specialist agents — originally built for WordPress agencies, now expanded to any URL.

**Pricing:**

- Free: $0 — 2 projects, 1 seat, 200 AI credits (one-time)
- Pro: $25/seat/mo annual · $29/seat/mo monthly — 5 projects, max 5 seats
- Business: $35/seat/mo annual · $42/seat/mo monthly — 10 projects, max 100 seats
- Enterprise: custom

**Best for:** WordPress agencies and creative teams that want AI-assisted design reviews, SEO audits, and accessibility checks baked into the feedback workflow.

**Strengths:**

- **AI InnerCircle** — six AI specialists (Pixel for design, Index for SEO, Navi for UX, etc.) analyze your project and generate actionable feedback automatically.
- **Unlimited guest/stakeholder access** on all plans — clients never need a paid seat.
- **Works on any URL** — not WordPress-only anymore, despite the origins.
- **Time tracking and Kanban board** built in — replaces a separate project management layer for small agencies.
- **Free plan** is genuinely usable for solo freelancers with 2 active projects.

**Weaknesses:**

- **Per-seat pricing** — a 5-person team on Pro costs $125/mo annual; not cheap for what is essentially a feedback tool.
- **White-label is Enterprise only** — don't assume it's available on Pro or Business.
- **AI complexity can be overkill** — the InnerCircle adds cognitive overhead for teams that just want visual comments.
- **WordPress roots show** — setup is smoother on WP sites; other stacks sometimes require extra configuration.
- **5-project cap on Pro** is tight for agencies running concurrent client work.

## 5. Gleap

**TL;DR:** An all-in-one customer support and feedback platform — bug reporting, live chat, AI chatbot, surveys, and roadmap in a single $149/mo flat-rate plan.

**Pricing:**

- Hobby: $39/mo (1 member, 1 project) · $31/mo annual
- Team: $149/mo unlimited members + projects · $119/mo annual — includes remove branding, custom domain, third-party integrations
- Enterprise: from $999/mo · $799/mo annual
- AI features billed per use (~$0.02/AI response)
- Email sending: $2/1,000 emails
- No free plan

**Best for:** SaaS teams that want to consolidate live chat, bug reporting, session replay, NPS surveys, and a public roadmap into one monthly bill.

**Strengths:**

- **Unlimited seats on Team plan** — $149/mo flat regardless of team size; no per-seat compounding.
- **Kai AI chatbot** answers user questions from your knowledge base 24/7 — reduces support load.
- **Session replay + console logs** captured automatically on bug reports.
- **iOS, Android, Flutter, React Native SDKs** — the strongest mobile coverage in this list.
- **Public roadmap with feature voting** built in — closes the loop from bug report to shipped feature.

**Weaknesses:**

- **No free plan** — Hobby at $39/mo is the floor, and it's limited to 1 member.
- **Overkill for pure website feedback** — if you just need clients to annotate a staging site, Gleap's full stack is more than you need.
- **AI costs add up** — $0.02/response sounds cheap; at scale it's a variable cost line you need to budget for.
- **Enterprise jumps to $999/mo** — the gap between Team and Enterprise is steep.

## 6. Ruttl

**TL;DR:** A lightweight visual feedback tool with a genuinely unique feature — live CSS editing directly on the page, so reviewers can show exact change values instead of describing them.

**Pricing:**

- Basic: Free — up to 5 users, 1 project, 5 pages, 1GB storage
- Pro: $18/user/mo — unlimited projects/pages, up to 50 users + 1 admin
- Business: $90/user/mo — unlimited admins + users, 100GB storage

**Best for:** Small design teams and freelancers who want to leave precise CSS change values for developers, not just vague annotations.

**Strengths:**

- **Live CSS editing** — unique in this category; reviewers can edit font sizes, colors, spacing directly on the page and share the exact values.
- **Free plan exists** — 1 project, 5 pages, 5 users; usable for a single client engagement.
- **No account required for clients** — share a link, they comment immediately.
- **Responsive preview** — switch between desktop, tablet, and mobile views while keeping comments visible.

**Weaknesses:**

- **Reliability issues reported** — multiple user reviews flag bugs with password-protected staging sites and inconsistent behavior on certain frameworks.
- **Per-user pricing** — $18/user/mo means a 5-person team costs $90/mo; Marker.io or Faster Fixes Agency are cheaper at that team size.
- **Shallow integrations** — Jira/Slack/ClickUp connectors create tasks with links back to Ruttl rather than syncing actual feedback data.
- **Free plan is severely limited** — 5 pages per project won't cover a real multi-page site review.

## 7. Markup.io

**TL;DR:** A flat-rate feedback tool for creative agencies that need to annotate not just websites, but also PDFs, images, videos, and HTML emails — 30+ file types in one workspace.

**Pricing:**

- Free plan: **discontinued January 2025**
- Pro: $79/mo flat — unlimited users, 1 workspace, unlimited markups, 500GB storage
- Enterprise: custom
- 30-day free trial available

**Best for:** Creative and marketing agencies that review mixed-format assets — websites, PDFs, video cuts, and design files — and want a single tool for all of them.

**Strengths:**

- **Flat-rate pricing** — $79/mo for unlimited users; no per-seat compounding regardless of team size.
- **30+ file types** — annotate websites, PDFs, images, videos, HTML emails, and more from one workspace.
- **No installation required** — works via URL proxy for websites; no script embed needed.
- **30-day trial** — the most generous trial window in this list.

**Weaknesses:**

- **Free plan was killed in January 2025** — if you're reading an older comparison that lists a free tier, it's wrong.
- **No developer-specific context** — no console logs, no DOM selectors, no React component tree; annotations are visual-only.
- **No self-hosted option.**
- **$79/mo is a significant jump** from the old free tier — teams that relied on the free plan had to absorb a 172%+ price increase.

## 8. Usersnap

**TL;DR:** An enterprise-grade feedback platform used by Red Hat, LEGO, and Harvard — strong on in-product micro-surveys and stakeholder feedback loops, but pricing is opaque.

**Pricing:**

- Free: first 20 feedback items only — effectively a trial, not a usable free tier
- Starter through Premium: **pricing not publicly displayed** — requires signup to see numbers
- Enterprise: custom

**Best for:** Enterprise product teams with dedicated product managers who need structured in-app surveys, user acceptance testing workflows, and stakeholder feedback portals.

**Strengths:**

- **Strong enterprise pedigree** — trusted by Red Hat, LEGO, Erste Bank, Harvard University.
- **In-product micro-surveys** — NPS, CSAT, CES, and custom surveys triggered by user behavior.
- **Stakeholder feedback portals** — structured review workflows for UAT and release sign-off.
- **Deep PM integrations** — Jira, Azure DevOps, GitHub, Slack, and more.

**Weaknesses:**

- **Pricing is hidden** — you can't evaluate cost without signing up; this is a genuine friction point for buyers doing a quick comparison.
- **Free tier is not a free tier** — 20 feedback items is a demo, not a plan.
- **Overkill for agency use** — the feature set is built around product teams, not client website reviews.
- **No self-hosted option.**

## 9. BugHerd

**TL;DR:** The original visual website feedback tool — still one of the best for non-technical client teams who need zero onboarding friction.

**Pricing:**

- Standard: $50/mo (5 members) · $42/mo annual
- Studio: $80/mo (10 members) · $67/mo annual
- Premium: $150/mo (25 members) · $125/mo annual
- Additional members: $8/seat/mo · $6.60/seat annual
- No free plan; 7-day trial
- Unlimited client users on all plans

**Best for:** Agencies with non-technical clients who need a proven, stable tool with zero learning curve — and don't mind paying for it.

**Strengths:**

- **Proven and stable** — BugHerd has been around since 2012; it works reliably and the UX is well-tested.
- **Unlimited client users** — clients can submit feedback without consuming a paid seat.
- **No Chrome extension required** — installs via a single JS snippet; clients just click and comment.
- **Kanban board built in** — feedback becomes tasks automatically, no separate project management tool needed.
- **Strong integration list** — Jira, Asana, ClickUp, Monday.com, Slack, GitHub, Zapier.

**Weaknesses:**

- **No free plan** — $50/mo minimum with a 7-day trial is a real commitment before you know if it fits.
- **Per-seat pricing after the base** — $8/seat/mo for member 6+ adds up quickly on larger teams.
- **No MCP/AI agent integration** — feedback doesn't connect to AI coding workflows.
- **No self-hosted option** — all data on BugHerd's infrastructure.
- **No session replay or console logs** — developers get a screenshot and URL, but not the full technical context that Marker.io or Userback provide.

## How to choose

The right tool depends on your team structure and workflow — not on feature checklists.

**You're a 3-person dev agency billing hourly → Faster Fixes Pro at $20/mo flat.** You get unlimited feedback, GitHub/Linear integration, white-label widget, and MCP server for AI-assisted fixes. The math is simple: $20/mo versus $50/mo minimum for BugHerd.

**You need structured bug reports flowing into Jira or Linear, with session replay → Marker.io.** The reporter model (free, unlimited client submissions) means your clients never need an account. The two-way Jira sync is the best in class. Budget $149/mo annual on the Team plan.

**You want zero vendor lock-in and own your data → Faster Fixes self-hosted.** The dashboard, API, and MCP server are AGPL-3.0. The widget packages are MIT. Deploy on your own infrastructure, keep all feedback data in your own database.

**You're a SaaS team that needs chat + bug reporting + roadmap in one bill → Gleap.** $149/mo flat for unlimited seats, with Kai AI chatbot, session replay, NPS surveys, and a public roadmap. It replaces 4–5 separate tools.

**You have non-technical clients who need zero onboarding → BugHerd or Marker.io.** BugHerd's UX is the most battle-tested for non-technical users. Marker.io's reporter model means clients submit via widget with no account at all.
